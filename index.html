Research

Research profile

My research aims to advance robust visual intelligence by combining representation learning, multimodal reasoning, and interactive system design. Across computer vision and multimodal AI, I am especially interested in methods that can bridge perception, structure, and use-oriented deployment.

A recurring theme in my work is to connect core algorithmic innovation with meaningful application settings, including digital fashion, wearable technologies, mobile systems, and visual content understanding. This allows research questions in real environments to inform new model design, supervision strategies, and evaluation protocols.

01

Structured Visual Understanding

Human parsing, pose estimation, landmark detection, and region- aware representation learning for people-centred visual analysis.

02

Multimodal Retrieval and Generation

Cross-modal search, sketch-based retrieval, multimodal LLM-based understanding, and generation with modality-aware reasoning.

03

Neural Rendering and 3D Computing

Novel-view synthesis, neural radiance fields, 3D Gaussian Splatting, virtual try-on, and animatable visual representations.

04

Human-centred Intelligent Systems

AI-enabled wearable systems, mobile interfaces, and intelligent applications that integrate technical methods with real-world use.
